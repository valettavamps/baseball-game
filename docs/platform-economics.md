# Platform Economics - Where the Money Goes

## Philosophy
**"The house always wins, but the players can win too."**

The platform needs to:
1. Pay founder/developers (you deserve it)
2. Cover operational costs (AI tokens, servers, etc.)
3. Keep enough in the ecosystem so players can profit
4. Sustain long-term growth (marketing, features, liquidity)

## Founder Compensation Model

### Option A: Fixed Salary + Profit Share
- **Salary:** $150K/year (guaranteed)
- **Profit share:** 50% of net profit after costs
- **Pro:** Stability + upside
- **Con:** Fixed burn even if revenue dips

### Option B: Pure Revenue Share
- **Take:** 30% of gross revenue
- **Pro:** Scales with success, no pressure in lean times
- **Con:** No guaranteed floor (risky early on)

### Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)
- **Year 1:** 30% of gross revenue (you're doing all the work)
- **Year 2+:** 25% of gross revenue (as team grows)
- **Exit milestone:** If platform hits $10M valuation, you lock in equity

**Example (Year 1):**
- Revenue: $1M
- Your cut: $300K
- After taxes/expenses: ~$200K take-home
- Not bad for a side project turned business! 💰

## Player Economics - Keeping It Sustainable

### The Balance
Players need to feel like they can make money, not just get extracted.

**Good economy:**
- 60% of stakers have positive ROI
- 20% break even
- 20% lose (bad team picks, over-leverage)

**Bad economy:**
- 90% of players lose money
- Only whales profit
- Death spiral (everyone quits)

### How to Keep 60% Profitable

1. **Inject rewards from platform fees**
   - 25% of revenue → Player Rewards Pool
   - Distributed as bonus APR, events, tournaments
   - Example: If protocol makes $1M, $250K goes back to players

2. **Dynamic difficulty balancing**
   - AI teams get better if too many humans are dominating
   - AI teams get worse if humans are struggling
   - Keeps competition balanced

3. **Tiered leagues**
   - Beginners start in "Rookie League" (lower stakes, lower fees)
   - Pros play in "Championship League" (high stakes, high fees)
   - Prevents whales steamrolling newbies

4. **Referral rewards**
   - Bring a friend → both get $50 in staking credits
   - Creates growth flywheel without diluting treasury

5. **Seasons with resets**
   - Every season = fresh start
   - Bad season? Next one is new opportunity
   - Prevents "rich get richer" forever

## Fee Philosophy - Not Being Greedy

### What Users Will Tolerate

**Fair fees (users accept):**
- 2.5% staking in/out (industry standard)
- 5% NFT sales (OpenSea charges 2.5%, we're higher but justified by utility)
- 1% loan origination (banks charge 3-5%)
- 10% auction house take (Christie's charges 25%!)

**Greedy fees (users revolt):**
- 10% staking in/out (too high, kills small stakes)
- 20% NFT sales (absurd)
- 5% loan origination (better to borrow elsewhere)

**The test:** If you wouldn't pay it yourself, don't charge it.

## Sustainability Features

### 1. Insurance Pool (From Treasury)
- Covers bad debt (loans that can't be repaid)
- Backstops stakers if team revenue collapses
- Builds trust ("we've got your back")

### 2. Dynamic Fee Adjustment
```
If treasury < $100K:
    fees increase 10% (temporarily)
    
If treasury > $500K:
    fees decrease 10% (reward users)
```

Auto-balancing keeps system healthy.

### 3. Burn Mechanism
- 20% of auction revenue → token burn
- Reduces supply over time
- Benefits all token holders (deflationary)

### 4. Community Grants
- 10% of treasury → fund community projects
- User-built tools, analytics, content
- Grows ecosystem value
